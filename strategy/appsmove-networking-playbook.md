# Appsmove — Networking Playbook (Doc 3 of 4) — Framework Reference

> Mechanism reference. Unicorn Studio's adaptation lives in `unicorn-positioning.md` (audience) and `unicorn-content-pillars.md` (community sharing).

## The Networking Principle

> Be the most helpful person in every room. Give away tools and resources that solve real problems. The business follows the generosity.

## Two Channels, One Goal

| Channel | Where | Advantage |
|---|---|---|
| Physical | JCI, IT communities, business events, meetups | Deep trust, memorable, strongest referral relationships |
| Digital | WhatsApp, Slack, Discord, Facebook | Scale, daily visibility, lead magnet distribution, global reach |

## Physical Networking Routine

| Frequency | Activity |
|---|---|
| Weekly | Attend 1–2 events or meetings |
| After every event | Message 3–5 people you met + send a lead magnet within 24hrs |
| Monthly | 4–6 one-on-one coffee chats or calls |
| Quarterly | Attend 1 larger conference or regional event |
| Ongoing | Volunteer, speak, present when possible |

## The Follow-Up Formula (within 24 hours of meeting someone)

1. Connect on LinkedIn with a personalized note referencing the conversation.
2. Send a lead magnet: "Hey [Name], great meeting you. We put together a free [guide/tool] — thought you might find it useful: [link]"
3. If you promised something (intro, resource, link), deliver within 24 hours.

## Digital Community Engagement Rules

1. **Listen first** (3–5 days): observe culture before contributing.
2. **Add value** before anything else.
3. **Share lead magnets and micro tools** naturally — no pitch.
4. **Build 1-on-1 relationships** — take interesting conversations to private chat.
5. **Never pitch services in groups.** Let your tools do the talking.

## The Community Rule: Zero Sales Angle

> In every community — WhatsApp, Slack, Discord, Facebook — you are a contributor, not a seller. Never mention pricing, services, or your offer directly. Share tools and resources that solve real problems. Buyers find them valuable, check your profile, and reach out on their own.

## Lead Magnets as Networking Currency

Deploy in:
- Physical events ("free guide on [topic] — want me to send it?" → get email)
- Community posts ("just finished a free [tool/guide], here's the link") — no pitch
- Follow-up messages
- To referral partners (something they can share)
- After coffee chats

## Strategic Referral Partnerships

| Partner type | Why they refer | Where to find |
|---|---|---|
| Adjacent service providers (SEO, branding, etc.) | Drive traffic but don't build/design | Slack communities, LinkedIn, events |
| Marketing consultants | Advise clients who need fulfillment support | LinkedIn, business events |
| CRM / automation specialists | Set up systems on sites they don't build | HubSpot/Salesforce communities |
| PPC / paid media | Drive leads to landing pages | Google/Meta partner communities |
| Business coaches | Advise founders who need scaling support | Coaching communities |

## 10 Networking Principles

1. Give first, always.
2. Follow up within 24 hours.
3. Lead with lead magnets.
4. Zero sales angle in communities.
5. Be consistent, not intense.
6. Listen more than you talk.
7. Play the long game.
8. Be memorable.
9. Connect others — make yourself the hub.
10. Track everything.

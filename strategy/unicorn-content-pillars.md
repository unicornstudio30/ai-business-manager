# Unicorn Studio — Content Pillars

Adapted from the Appsmove content strategy (mechanism), reframed for Unicorn's audience (AI SaaS founders + B2B SMBs adopting AI).

## Audience

Primary: AI SaaS founders (early-stage to growth) shipping AI products.
Secondary: B2B SMB owners drowning in manual ops who know AI is the answer but don't know how to start.
Tertiary: Tech leaders at established companies whose teams are quietly installing AI piecemeal.

## The 5 Content Pillars

### 1. The AI Gap
**Why it works**: Names a fear they already feel. Sparks urgency without manipulation.
**Examples**:
- "Your competitor installed AI last quarter. You'd never know unless you tried to compete on speed."
- "Most SaaS teams underestimate the prompt iteration cycle, not the integration code."
- "The honest version: businesses your size are quietly automating right now."

### 2. Custom Beats Templates
**Why it works**: Differentiates from no-code AI tools and AI agencies that resell GPT wrappers.
**Examples**:
- "I just saw the 4th lookalike AI agency this week — same Zapier-style stack, same deck."
- "We don't pick from a menu. Every workflow gets scoped from your operation, not from a template."
- "If your AI deploys to OUR cloud, you don't own your AI. We deploy to yours."

### 3. Behind the Build
**Why it works**: Proves competence by showing the actual mechanism. Builds trust faster than testimonials.
**Examples**:
- "How we cut a SaaS founder's onboarding ops from 4 hours/customer to 11 minutes."
- "The 7 things we put in EVERY scope doc before we start building."
- "Why we ship in 5–8 weeks and what the timeline actually looks like."

### 4. Why Capacity is Capped
**Why it works**: Real scarcity (3-4 clients/month) is differentiated positioning. Most agencies try to look bigger than they are. We do the opposite.
**Examples**:
- "We onboard 3-4 new clients a month. Here's why the math doesn't work if we take more."
- "I turned down a $40K project last week. The fit wasn't right and our queue is honest."
- "Why we never compete on price — capacity is the constraint."

### 5. Founder Lessons
**Why it works**: Makes the operator visible. AI buyers buy from operators, not agencies.
**Examples**:
- "Two years ago I said yes to every project. It almost killed the business."
- "The one question we now ask on every discovery call that's saved us 6 bad clients."
- "What I learned from the worst delivery of my career."

## 6 Content Types (from Appsmove framework, still apply)

1. **Contrarian** — Position most disagree with. Sparks debate.
2. **Curiosity Gap** — Open loop the reader needs closed.
3. **Number/Revenue** — Lead with a specific, concrete number.
4. **Story Open** — Drop the reader mid-scene.
5. **Confession** — Admit a mistake or weakness.
6. **Direct Address** — Speak directly to one segment ("AI SaaS founders:").

## 3 Hook Styles

1. **Pattern Interrupt** — Breaks scrolling rhythm.
2. **Specific Tension** — Names a concrete conflict.
3. **Implicit Promise** — Signals payoff without spelling it out.

## Content Calendar — Notion `Type` field mapping

Use these Type values when writing to the Notion Content Calendar:

- **Linkedin Text Post** — most stories, contrarians, direct address
- **Linkedin Carousel** — pricing/process walkthroughs, "Behind the Build" deep dives
- **Linkedin news announce** — capacity / new service launches
- **Blog Post** — long-form Behind the Build, framework explainers
- **Tweet** / **Twitter thread** — contrarians + numbers
- **Newsletter** — weekly digest version
- **Sponsored Post** — paid amplification of best performers

## Sprint cadence

Two 12-hour content sprints per month:
- Sprint 1: cover Weeks 1-2 (15 posts)
- Sprint 2: cover Weeks 3-4 (15 posts)

Each sprint balances pillars:
- Pillar 1 (AI Gap): 6 posts/month
- Pillar 2 (Custom Beats Templates): 6 posts/month
- Pillar 3 (Behind the Build): 8 posts/month
- Pillar 4 (Why Capacity is Capped): 4 posts/month
- Pillar 5 (Founder Lessons): 6 posts/month

## Framework field

In Notion's `Framework` field per post, use one of:
- `Story (5-part)` — Hook → Tension → Turning Point → Outcome → Takeaway
- `Contrarian (5-part)` — Claim → Common view → Counter-evidence → Reframe → Invitation
- `Trend Commentary` — Trend → Mainstream reaction → What you see → Your take → CTA
- `Myth-bust` — Myth → Origin → Reality → Reframe
- `Carousel (deep dive)` — 8-12 slides, one idea per slide
- `Number-led` — Hook with a number → Mechanism → Lesson

# Appsmove — Business Systems (Doc 4 of 4) — Framework Reference

> Note: This is the original Appsmove framework reference. Unicorn Studio's adapted version lives in `unicorn-positioning.md` and `unicorn-sales-playbook.md`. Use this for the *mechanism* — pipeline structure, project lifecycle, financial ops, scaling rules.

## Pipeline Stages (10-stage Appsmove model)

1. Prospect — identified, not contacted → contact within 24hrs
2. Contacted — first outreach sent → follow up Day 4
3. Engaged — responded, conversation active → move toward call
4. Call Booked — discovery call scheduled → prepare and research
5. First Project — first project quoted/accepted → trigger onboarding
6. Repeat Partner — sent 2+ projects → nurture toward volume
7. Volume Partner — 3+ projects/month (10% discount) → maintain and upsell
8. Scaled Partner — 5+ projects/month (15% discount) → priority service
9. Nurture — not ready now → monthly touchpoint
10. Lost — declined or unresponsive → revisit quarterly

## Daily CRM Routine

1. Log every interaction (5 min) — every DM, email, call, comment.
2. Update pipeline stages (5 min) — move contacts as conversations progress.
3. Queue follow-ups (5 min) — never let an active lead go 7+ days without a touch.
4. Weekly review (15 min, Sundays) — pipeline health, conversion rates, partner distribution.

## Project Lifecycle

| Phase | What happens | Timeline | Owner |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1. Brief Received | Partner submits project | Day 0 | Partner |
| 2. Scope Review | Confirm service, tier, pages, price | Day 0–1 | You |
| 3. Assignment | Assign designer/developer | Day 1 | You / Team |
| 4. Design (if applicable) | Figma/PSD per brief | Day 1–5 | Designer |
| 5. Development | WordPress build | Day 1–7 | Developer |
| 6. QA | Responsiveness, speed, cross-browser, links, forms | Before delivery | QA / You |
| 7. Partner Review | Staging link or files + walkthrough | Per turnaround | Partner |
| 8. Revisions | 2 rounds included | 1–2 days/round | Designer / Dev |
| 9. Final Delivery | Source files, go-live, handoff | Same day | You |

## Communication SLAs

| Situation | Response time | Channel |
|---|---|---|
| New brief | Within 2 hours | Slack / WhatsApp |
| Partner question | Within 4 hours (business hours) | Slack / WhatsApp |
| Project status update | Proactive at 50% completion | Slack + Loom |
| Issue / blocker | Immediately + propose solution | Slack / Call |
| Staging / file delivery | Send link + walkthrough | Email + Loom |
| Revision feedback received | Acknowledge within 2 hours | Slack |
| Urgent / emergency | Within 30 minutes | Phone / WhatsApp |

## Weekly Business Review (Sundays, 30–45 min)

1. Pipeline review: prospects at each stage, bottlenecks, next actions
2. Partner health: anyone going quiet? Unhappy? Proactive outreach.
3. Revenue check: invoiced this week, outstanding, projected
4. Delivery status: projects in progress, any at risk of missing turnaround
5. Capacity: can your team handle current volume + incoming projects?
6. Partner growth: anyone ready to move from Repeat to Volume? Volume to Scaled?
7. Content + networking: posts going out? Communities engaged? Lead magnets shared?
8. Next week priorities: top 3 things to accomplish

## The Systems Principle

> If it happens more than twice, systematize it. If it's in your head, document it. If only you can do it, that's a bottleneck.

## The 3X Rule

> Every time you do something for the third time, stop and document it. Record a Loom, write the steps, save it.

## When to Hire

| Signal | Hire | How |
|---|---|---|
| Turning down projects / slow turnaround | Additional WP developer(s) | Start freelancer, then full-time |
| Design backlog growing | Additional designer | Per-project freelancer first |
| 2+ hrs/day on partner communication | Project coordinator | Part-time, train on templates |
| QA slipping or bugs post-delivery | Dedicated QA person | Per-project or part-time |
| Admin (invoicing, scheduling) eating 1+ hrs/day | Virtual assistant | 10–20 hrs/week |

## Processes to Document First

1. Partner onboarding (welcome message → first project delivery)
2. Project intake (brief received → assignment)
3. Design workflow (brief → Figma/PSD delivery)
4. Dev workflow (design/brief → WordPress build)
5. QA checklist (every check before partner delivery)
6. Communication templates (updates, delivery, revisions)
7. Invoicing and payment follow-up
8. White-label file naming and delivery packaging

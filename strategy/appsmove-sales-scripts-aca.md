# Appsmove — Sales Scripts (ACA Framework Edition) — Framework Reference

> Mechanism reference. Unicorn Studio's adapted scripts are in `unicorn-sales-playbook.md`. Use this for the *ACA framework* itself — Acknowledge, Compliment, Ask — applied to every channel.

## The ACA Framework

From Alex Hormozi's $100M Leads. Replaces generic, pitch-first DMs with messages that earn trust.

- **A — Acknowledge**: Specific reference to them. Their post, their company, recent win, role. Proves you actually looked.
- **C — Compliment**: Tie to a positive trait. Recognition, not flattery.
- **A — Ask**: Question that opens conversation. NEVER pitch in message 1. Indirect ask beats direct ask.

## Golden Rules

1. EVERY response follows ACA — even replies inside a conversation.
2. Never pitch in the first message. Goal of message 1 = get a reply.
3. Use the indirect ask: "Do you know any [X] who struggle with [Y]?" — they'll self-identify if they need help.
4. Speed is king: respond in minutes, not hours.
5. Voice memos > text for breaking a conversation pattern.
6. Volume wins: 100 outreach touches/day is the target.

## ACA Cheat Sheet (memorize)

| Situation | Acknowledge | Compliment | Ask |
|---|---|---|---|
| Cold DM (Day 1) | Their specific work | Their quality/standards | To connect (no pitch) |
| Cold DM (Day 4) | A growth signal | Difficulty of what they do | How they handle [their domain] |
| Cold DM (Day 7) | New observation | Their momentum | If [problem] matters |
| Cold DM (Day 14) | Their trajectory | Their focus | Leave landing page link |
| Warm DM (post commenter) | Their comment | Their company | [Domain] question |
| Warm DM (profile viewer) | Profile view | Their work quality | Current focus |
| Cold Email #1 | Found their company | Portfolio quality | Capacity need? |
| Cold Email #2 | Busy schedule | Building something real | Case study relevance |
| Cold Email #3 | Silence understood | Success = busy | File close + link |
| Referral Ask | Great delivery | Connected to operators | Know 1–2 people? |
| Discovery Call | Each answer | Their effort/thinking | Next diagnostic Q |
| Objection | Valid concern | Shows they care | Reframe + next step |

## Day 1 — Connection Note (LinkedIn)

```
Hey [Name], just came across [Company]'s work on [specific].

[ACK] Looks like you're doing some seriously sharp [domain] work.
[COMP] The [specific detail: portfolio / direction / client roster] says a lot about the standards you hold.
[ASK] Would love to connect — I'm in the [adjacent space] and always enjoy learning how [their type] approach growth.
```

## Day 4 — Value-First DM (after connection accepted)

```
Hey [Name], thanks for connecting.

[ACK] Noticed [Company] has been growing — saw [specific signal: case study, hire, win, posting cadence].
[COMP] Scaling [their work] while keeping quality high is one of the hardest things to do. Clearly you're figuring it out.
[ASK] Quick question — how are you currently handling [their relevant domain] for [their projects]? In-house, freelancers, or outsourcing?

Just curious — it's a topic that comes up a lot in conversations I have with [their type].
```

## Day 4 — The Indirect Ask (alternative)

```
Hey [Name],

[ACK] Been following [Company]'s posts — your take on [topic] was spot on.
[COMP] You clearly know the [their game] better than most. I can tell you're connected to some serious operators.
[ASK] Random question — do you know any [their type] struggling with [problem you solve]? We help [audience] with [outcome] and I'm looking for the right [partners/clients].

No pressure at all — just thought you might know someone.
```

## Day 7 — Follow-Up

```
Hey [Name], totally get it if the last message got buried.

[ACK] Saw you just [posted X / launched Y / shared Z] — that's awesome.
[COMP] Clearly you're not slowing down. That kind of momentum is rare.
[ASK] If [domain capacity] is ever something that eats into your time or margins, I'd love to share how we help [audience]. Would that be useful, or is your setup already dialed in?
```

## Day 10 — Email with Mini Case Study

```
Subject: How a [size]-person [type] added $XK without hiring

Hey [Name],

[ACK] I know you're building something real with [Company] — your [portfolio / clients / content] makes that clear.
[COMP] Most [their type] I talk to are struggling to keep up. The fact that you're still growing says a lot.
[ASK] Quick story: a [similar type] was losing [opportunity] because they couldn't [deliver/scale]. After partnering with us behind the scenes, they added [outcome] in [timeframe] without [hiring/expanding].

If that resonates, I'd love to share how the model works. 15 minutes?

If not — genuinely wishing [Company] all the best.
```

## Day 14 — Final DM (Respectful Close)

```
Hey [Name], last one from me — I respect your time too much to keep showing up uninvited.

[ACK] I can see [Company] is on a great trajectory. You're clearly building something that matters.
[COMP] That takes a level of focus most people don't have. Seriously.
[ASK] If you ever hit a point where [domain capacity] becomes the bottleneck — overflow, big project, just wanting your time back — here's how we work: [landing page]

No hard feelings either way.
```

## ACA Response Pattern (every reply in a conversation)

When they say they use freelancers / in-house / not now → ALWAYS:

```
[ACK] Restate their answer in your own words — show active listening.
[COMP] Tie to a positive trait — what does this say about how they operate?
[ASK] The next diagnostic question.
```

## Objection Handling — ACA Style

| Objection | ACA Response |
|---|---|
| "We've been burned before" | [A] That's completely valid. [C] The fact that you're cautious means you take your clients seriously. [A] That's exactly why we guarantee the first project — built and running, or we work free. Fair? |
| "Your price is too high" | [A] I hear you. [C] You clearly know your margins. [A] What's the cost of staying [in current state] for another 6 months? If that number's higher than our scope, the math's straightforward. |
| "We're not ready right now" | [A] Totally fair. [C] Sounds like things are running smooth. [A] Can I check back in 30 days? And do you know anyone else feeling the [problem] right now? |
| "We need to think about it" | [A] Of course. [C] I'd be concerned if you didn't think carefully. [A] What specifically would help you decide? Happy to send a case study, reference, or portfolio. |
| "We have someone in-house" | [A] Makes sense. [C] Building an in-house [team] is a real investment. [A] A lot of our partners started the same way and use us for overflow. If you ever hit capacity, we're here. |

## Discovery Call ACA

Use ACA throughout. After every answer the prospect gives:

- Acknowledge: restate what they said in your own words.
- Compliment: tie to a positive character trait ("That takes real discipline").
- Ask: next diagnostic question.

The framework keeps the conversation flowing and prevents the call from feeling like an interrogation.

## Comment Selling — ACA in Public

Every comment on a prospect's post = ACA structure. The comment is a public ACA pattern interrupt.

```
[A] [Specific reference to their post — what they argued, the example they used]
[C] [Tie to a trait — operational discipline, taste, clarity]
[A] [Sharp follow-up question that drives engagement]
```

3–5 sentences. Substantive. Public. No pitch. Goal: name recognition before the DM.

# Appsmove — Sales System (Doc 2 of 4) — Framework Reference

> Mechanism reference. Unicorn Studio's adapted scripts and ICP are in `unicorn-sales-playbook.md` and `unicorn-positioning.md`.

## The Core Principle

> Sales is a volume game with a quality filter. You need enough conversations happening at once that you're never waiting on one prospect to decide. But every touch should be personalized, relevant, and value-first. All outreach uses the ACA Framework.

## Daily Sales Block — Priority Order

| Priority | Activity | Purpose |
|---|---|---|
| First | Cold outbound (new DMs + emails) | Fill the top of the funnel |
| Second | Follow-ups on active conversations | Move leads through pipeline |
| Third | Warm outreach (content engagers, profile viewers) | Convert warm attention |
| Last | CRM updates + pipeline review + list building | Maintenance |

Cold outbound first when energy is highest. Follow-ups next because most deals are won there. CRM is last because it's maintenance, not revenue-generating.

## Throughout the Day: Comment Selling

Outside the outreach block, leave valuable ACA comments on prospects' LinkedIn posts.

**The Comment Selling Playbook**:
1. Build a "Hot 50" list — prospects you want to engage.
2. Comment on their posts regularly — 3–5 sentences, substantive.
3. Never pitch in comments.
4. Layer with DM sequence after several comments over 1–2 weeks.
5. Rotate the list periodically.

## Channel 1: Cold DMs — LinkedIn 7-Step Sequence

| Step | Action | Channel | Purpose |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Connection request + personalized note | LinkedIn | Open the door |
| 2 | Like/comment on 2–3 of their recent posts | LinkedIn | Get on their radar |
| 3 | Value-first DM | LinkedIn | Start a real conversation |
| 4 | Follow up if no response | LinkedIn | Gentle bump |
| 5 | Personalized email with case study + landing page link | Email | Different channel |
| 6 | Final DM with direct ask | LinkedIn | Clear CTA for a call |
| 7 | Move to nurture sequence (monthly touchpoints) | LinkedIn / Email | Stay top of mind |

## Channel 1b: Facebook Group 8-Step Sequence

| Step | Action | Channel | Purpose |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Friend request (no message) | Facebook | Get on their radar |
| 2 | Engage with posts/comments in group | Facebook Group | Build recognition |
| 3 | React to personal profile posts | Facebook | Show genuine interest |
| 4 | Warm DM referencing the group | Messenger | Real conversation with context |
| 5 | Personalized bump | Messenger | Reference NEW signal |
| 6 | Voice note or short video DM with offer | Messenger | Stand out |
| 7 | Breakup message with landing page link | Messenger | Final touch |
| 8 | Move to nurture | Facebook | Periodic engagement |

> Voice notes and video DMs are the secret weapon on Facebook — almost nobody uses them in B2B outreach.

## Channel 2: Cold Email — Infrastructure

- Separate domain for outreach (protect main domain deliverability)
- Warm up domain 2–3 weeks before sending volume
- Send from personal email (firstname@domain), not info@/hello@
- Keep volume per inbox low to avoid spam filters

## Channel 3: Warm Outreach Triggers

- Post commenters → DM with ACA reply referencing their comment
- Profile viewers → connect with note
- Engaged in same group → DM referencing the conversation
- Lead magnet downloaders → personal DM within 24–48hrs

## Channel 4: Referral Engine

- Ask after every successful delivery: "Glad you're happy! Do you know 1–2 others I should connect with?"
- Build referral partners (non-competing): SEO, branding, copy, CRM, paid media, business coaches.
- Make it easy: draft a short intro message they can forward.
- Reciprocal referrals + tangible incentive.

## Channel 5: Inbound from Website

- Calendly embed — reduce friction to zero.
- Live chat for questions.
- Short explainer video (60–90s, you to camera).
- Show real results (number of partners, projects, on-time %).
- Capacity scarcity if real ("we take X new partners per month").

## Channel 6: Automated Community Outreach

> This is your force multiplier. Scrape public WhatsApp / Slack / Discord groups → auto-join → scrape members → run automated lead-magnet campaigns → warm leads enter manual pipeline.

(Note: Treat automation carefully — TOS for these platforms is strict. Manual community participation usually wins long-term.)

## Discovery Call Framework

| Phase | Duration | What to do |
|---|---|---|
| 1. Build Rapport | 2–3 min | Their journey, their specialty |
| 2. Diagnose | 8–10 min | Discovery questions; let them talk |
| 3. Share Experience | 3–4 min | One story of a partner in similar situation |
| 4. Present Offer | 2–3 min | Starter project + guarantee |
| 5. Close or Next Step | 1–2 min | "Want to try a project together?" |

## Discovery Questions

1. "How are you handling [their core problem] for client projects right now?"
2. "What's working about your current setup, and what's not?"
3. "Have you ever turned down a project because you didn't have capacity?"
4. "How much time do you personally spend managing X vs. selling?"
5. "If you had a [team/system] you could fully trust, what would change?"
6. "What's your biggest concern about working with a [partner like us]?" ← **most important — that's the objection**

## Pipeline Stages (8-stage minimum)

1. Prospect — identified, not contacted
2. Contacted — first outreach sent
3. Engaged — they responded
4. Call Booked — discovery scheduled
5. Proposal / Starter — offered the guarantee starter
6. Won — deal closed
7. Nurture — not ready now
8. Lost — full sequence done, no response

## Daily CRM Routine

- Log every conversation (DM, email, call, comment).
- Move contacts between stages.
- Queue tomorrow's follow-ups.

## Weekly Review

- New prospects entered the pipeline?
- Conversations turned into calls?
- Calls converted into projects?
- Where's the drop-off? Fix the weakest link.
- Replenish prospect list for next week.
- Refine DM/email templates based on what's working.
- Ask current partners for referrals.

## Monthly System Maintenance

- Refresh "Hot 50" comment selling list.
- Build new batch of prospect targets.
- Review which channels produce the most calls.
- Update landing page with new testimonials.
- Refresh automated community campaigns with new lead magnets.
